Marsa Alam on the Red Sea

back: Karnak Sunset over the Red Sea from Marsa Alam  

21-22 Nov 2010, Red Sea, Egypt
-------------------------------------------
Decided not to risk travel to the Sinai Penninsula, and headed instead to the Red Sea coast. Marsa Alam, located some 400 km south of the tip of the Sinai, has been reported to have decent snorkling. The bus dumped us at one end of town about two hours later than advertised. Started walking to the other end where Emy Lodge is located. It's not a very large town; most extablishments are concentrated on the beach side of the main road, with generous spacing between them.
-------------------------------------------

 Found the Emy Lodge, and it looked inviting, yet reasonably priced. As soon as my pack was on the floor of the cabin, headed back to the office where meals coud be ordered. I'm hungry after that long bus ride accross the Arabian Desert to the coast. The meal was so good, had to stop and take a photo.
View from the common area while eating my early dinner. Not too many guests at this time of year, even though the weather is perfect. The plan is to relax and walk around town this evening, but get an early start tomorrow and check out the snorkling. Noticed some dive shops on the way in, which should be ideal for information.

This lounge area is just outside the office and kitchen facilities ... sure to rock during the summer "high season". Was I the only guest today?
View of the Red Sea from the common area. Snorkled paralell to the beach in three locations. All were nice, but not fantastic. Mostly hard corals and small, but colorful fish. Along the beach, most of the dive shops were closed, or the crew off on a dive boat. It may be nice to have the place to yourself, but only after you know 'where to go'.

Have a nice day, and start planning your trip to Egypt!
